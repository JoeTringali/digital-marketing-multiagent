# Digital Marketing Best Practices Reference

A quick-reference guide used internally by the specialist agents.

---

## Market Research
- Always define at least 3 buyer personas before launching any campaign.
- Use **Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)** to understand motivation, not just demographics.
- Validate assumptions with primary data (surveys, interviews) before scaling spend.
- Tools: Google Trends, SparkToro, SEMrush Market Explorer, Statista, SimilarWeb.

---

## Website Presence
- Landing pages should have a single, clear CTA above the fold.
- Page load time under 2.5 seconds (Largest Contentful Paint) is the gold standard.
- Use heatmaps (Hotjar / Microsoft Clarity) to identify drop-off points.
- Trust signals (testimonials, reviews, security badges) directly lift conversion rates.
- A/B test one element at a time; run tests for statistical significance (≥95%).

---

## Email Marketing
- Welcome emails have 4× the open rate of standard campaigns — always send one.
- Keep list hygiene: remove unengaged subscribers every 90 days.
- Mobile-first design: >60% of emails are opened on mobile.
- Subject lines under 50 characters perform best on mobile.
- Deliverability checklist: SPF ✓, DKIM ✓, DMARC ✓, custom sending domain ✓.

---

## Copywriting
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature ("Save 3 hours a week" not "Has automation").
- Use the **PAS framework** (Problem → Agitate → Solution) for high-converting copy.
- Write at a 6th–8th grade reading level for broad audiences.
- Every piece of copy needs one clear CTA. Multiple CTAs dilute action.
- Test at least 3 headline variants per campaign.

---

## SEO
- Focus on **search intent** first: match content type to informational / commercial / transactional queries.
- Build topic clusters (pillar page + supporting posts) to establish topical authority.
- Core Web Vitals are a confirmed Google ranking signal — prioritise LCP, FID, CLS.
- Internal linking is underrated: link new content to established high-authority pages.
- Aim for 1 high-quality backlink per 1,000 words of content created.

---

## YouTube Marketing
- The first 30 seconds of a video are critical — hook viewers immediately.
- Custom thumbnails improve CTR by 30–50% vs auto-generated.
- Chapters (timestamps) improve watch time and appear in Google search results.
- Posting consistency matters more than frequency: 1× week beats sporadic daily uploads.
- End screens and cards drive channel subscriptions and cross-video watch time.

## LinkedIn Marketing
- Personal profiles outperform company pages by 5–10× on organic reach.
- Carousels (PDF slides) are the highest-engagement format on LinkedIn.
- Post without external links in the body — add the link in the first comment.
- Engage with comments within the first 60 minutes to boost algorithmic reach.
- Lead-gen forms convert 2–3× better than sending traffic to a landing page.

---

## App Marketing
- ASO title keyword placement has the highest ranking weight — choose carefully.
- First 3 screenshots in the store listing make or break install conversion.
- Day-1 retention below 25% indicates an onboarding problem, not an acquisition problem.
- Push notification opt-in rate: ask after delivering value, not at first launch.
- LTV:CAC ratio should be ≥3:1 for sustainable paid UA.

---

## Google Ads
- Quality Score of 7+ reduces CPC by up to 28%.
- Negative keywords are as important as positive keywords.
- Performance Max requires 4–6 weeks of learning before optimising bids.
- Smart Bidding needs ≥30 conversions/month per campaign to work effectively.
- Always link Google Ads ↔ GA4 ↔ Google Search Console for full data visibility.

---

## Facebook / Meta Ads
- Creative is responsible for ~70% of ad performance (Meta's own research).
- Refresh creatives every 2–3 weeks to combat ad fatigue (monitor Frequency >2.5).
- Broad targeting + strong creative often outperforms narrow audience targeting post-iOS 14.
- Conversion campaigns need ≥50 conversion events/week per ad set for stable delivery.
- Always use Conversions API (CAPI) alongside Pixel to recover lost signal.

---

## Google Analytics (GA4)
- Set up GA4 **before** launching any campaigns — retroactive data cannot be recovered.
- Use consistent UTM parameters: `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content`.
- Mark all key actions as Conversions in GA4, not just purchases.
- Explore report > Funnel exploration is the fastest way to spot drop-off.
- Enable Google Signals for cross-device reporting (requires consent where required by law).
